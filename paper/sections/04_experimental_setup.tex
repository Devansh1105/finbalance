\section{Experimental Setup}
\label{sec:setup}

\paragraph{Models and decoding.}
We evaluate six frontier LLMs through a single API gateway: Gemini~3 Flash (anchor; full ablation matrix), GPT-5 at low reasoning effort, Claude Haiku~4.5, Grok-4.3, DeepSeek V3.2, and Qwen~3 235B. All six receive the full core evaluation split of $n{=}143$ at baseline; verifier replication is full on Gemini, Claude Haiku, and DeepSeek, partial on Qwen ($n{=}85$, standard records only). Decoding uses temperature $0.0$ with \texttt{max\_tokens} of $8192$ for prompt/visibility ablations and $12000$ for tool variants; the best-of-3 selection condition is the sole stochastic configuration (temperature $0.7$, three independent samples). Submissions are parsed by a strict JSON parser with thousand-separator repair; unparseable submissions score zero and are reported separately.

\paragraph{Ablations and bootstrap.}
Each evaluation is an \texttt{AblationSpec} triple. The matrix on the anchor model spans five prompt variants (baseline, guided private-solve, self-check, doc-refs-strict, balance-reconstruction), ten visibility variants (full packet; evidence-only oracle; $+5{,}+15{,}+30$ distractors; evidence-last; OCR-only; no-allowed-accounts; no-distractors oracle; support-docs-removed), and seven tool variants (no-tools, calculator, document-search, ledger-check, full-tool agent, \verifier{} 1-pass, \verifier{} 2-pass, and the best-of-3 condition). The full grid is in Appendix~\ref{appx:ablation-grid}. For each treatment we compute a paired bootstrap ($n{=}5000$ resamples over a shared record-id set) of six metrics (\bsexact{}, \bsrecon{}, strict and lenient joint match, \incCode{}, strict \texttt{doc\_refs} mismatch rate); CIs not crossing zero are labelled significant. A complete baseline run costs $\sim$\$0.90 on the anchor model; a \verifier{} 1-pass run costs $\sim$\$1.85; the full ablation matrix totals $\sim$\$15--\$20.

\paragraph{Human validation.}
\label{sec:human-validation}
We released a 75-record expert-validation packet spanning all eight industries, all five difficulty levels, and 15 inconsistency codes. Each record was rendered as a self-contained Markdown file with the full document packet, opening trial balance, ground-truth entries, ground-truth balance sheet, and a verification form plus overall verdict. Reviewers were practising finance professionals at two major U.S.\ investment banks and worked only from the Markdown---no code, no JSON. The intended assignment covers all 75 records once: Reviewer~1 records 1--50 and Reviewer~2 records 1--25 plus 51--75, with records 1--25 independently double-reviewed for agreement. On that overlap, raw agreement is $92\%$ on document analogy, $92\%$ on entry correctness, $100\%$ on entry completeness, $92\%$ on \texttt{doc\_refs} support, $100\%$ on difficulty calibration, and $84\%$ on the overall verdict. Cohen's $\kappa$ is not informative because the overlap has highly skewed positive marginals; we therefore report raw agreement and per-reviewer marginals in Appendix~\ref{appx:human-protocol}. Full forms, the protocol, and per-record transcripts are released as supplementary material.
