\begin{abstract}
Existing financial NLP benchmarks evaluate question answering over
pre-existing reports, leaving untested a harder skill that real
accounting demands: constructing an internally consistent balance
sheet from raw journal entries.
\benchmark{} fills this gap with 2{,}500 synthetic yet
accounting-realistic problems across five difficulty levels,
scored against deterministic ledger-derived ground truth.
In a controlled six-model study, GPT-5.2 with \CoT{} reaches
\fbs{}~$=90.85$ and perfect balance accuracy, while DeepSeek-v3.2
under \zs{} exposes a striking structural--numerical split:
it recalls $96.8\%$ of expected accounts (\acr{}) yet satisfies the
accounting equation only $15\%$ of the time (\ba{}), indicating
fluent account naming without sound arithmetic.
\end{abstract}
