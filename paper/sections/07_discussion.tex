\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}

The central finding is not that frontier LLMs cannot do accounting. It is that capable models produce locally correct postings and then fail to maintain global consistency between those postings, their citations, and the balance sheet they report. The verifier shows that much of this residual error is process-level: when a deterministic global check exists, a single feedback turn recovers substantially more than best-of-3 selection.

This does not make verifier feedback a universal recipe. Accounting is unusual because the ledger that produces labels \emph{is} the verifier; tasks without such a specification require learned verifiers \citep{cobbe2021gsm8k,lightman2024verify} or LM-as-feedback \citep{madaan2023selfrefine,shinn2023reflexion}. The inconsistency-code degradation is the warning sign: feedback optimized for ledger consistency can bias a model away from saying that the evidence itself is contradictory.
