\section{Appendix}

\subsection{Worked Pipeline Example}
\label{app:worked-example}

This appendix gives a concrete end-to-end example of the benchmark
pipeline, from prompted input to ledger processing to the expected
JSON answer.

\paragraph{Prompted input.}
\begin{verbatim}
Opening balances:
Cash 49,400; Owner's Equity 49,400

Transactions:
1. Dr Equipment 17,700 / Cr Cash 17,700
2. Dr Operating Expenses 200 / Cr Cash 200
3. Dr Prepaid Rent 5,900 / Cr Cash 5,900
4. Dr Prepaid Rent 3,400 / Cr Cash 3,400
5. Dr Cash 20,600 /
   Cr Owner's Equity 20,600
\end{verbatim}

\paragraph{Ledger processing.}
\begin{center}
\small
\begin{tabularx}{\columnwidth}{@{}l>{\raggedright\arraybackslash}X@{}}
\toprule
Step & Running balances and effect \\
\midrule
Opening & Cash \(=49{,}400\); Owner's Equity \(=49{,}400\). \\
Tx 1 & Purchase equipment for cash. Cash decreases to \(31{,}700\); Equipment increases to \(17{,}700\). \\
Tx 2 & Record operating expense. Cash decreases to \(31{,}500\); Operating Expenses \(=200\). \\
Tx 3 & Record prepaid rent. Cash decreases to \(25{,}600\); Prepaid Rent \(=5{,}900\). \\
Tx 4 & Record additional prepaid rent. Cash decreases to \(22{,}200\); Prepaid Rent increases to \(9{,}300\). \\
Tx 5 & Owner contributes additional cash. Cash increases to \(42{,}800\); Owner's Equity increases to \(70{,}000\). \\
Closing & Expense accounts close into equity. Operating Expenses \(=200\) therefore become Retained Earnings \(=-200\). \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabularx}
\end{center}

\paragraph{Expected output.}
\begin{verbatim}
{
  "assets": {
    "Cash": 42800,
    "Equipment": 17700,
    "Prepaid Rent": 9300
  },
  "liabilities": {},
  "equity": {
    "Owner's Equity": 70000,
    "Retained Earnings": -200
  }
}
\end{verbatim}

The final statement balances because total assets equal
\(42{,}800 + 17{,}700 + 9{,}300 = 69{,}800\), while total equity
equals \(70{,}000 - 200 = 69{,}800\) and liabilities are zero.
This example makes explicit the accounting behaviors the benchmark
tests: transaction accumulation, section placement, and closing of
income-statement activity into Retained Earnings.

\subsection{Evaluation Protocol Details}

The benchmark uses a deterministic synthetic generator, a stratified held-out split, and structured JSON scoring.
All reported runs ask the model to return three top-level sections: \texttt{assets}, \texttt{liabilities}, and \texttt{equity}.
If a response contains reasoning or markdown wrappers, the parser extracts the final parseable JSON object before scoring.

\subsection{Prompted Output Format}

All reported runs ask the model to emit a JSON object with three top-level keys:
\texttt{assets}, \texttt{liabilities}, and \texttt{equity}.
\CoT{} prompts may include intermediate reasoning, but the final score is based only on the terminal structured answer.

\subsection{Concrete Model Failure Examples}
\label{app:failure-examples}

The following examples are drawn verbatim from retained per-problem
outputs.  Each illustrates a distinct failure mode discussed in the
main paper.

\paragraph{Arithmetic collapse.}
DeepSeek-v3.2 under \zs{} on problem FB\_3\_01855 (Level~3) recalls
every expected account (\acr{}\,$=1.0$) yet fails the balance equation
(\ba{}\,$=0$, \textsc{BEM}\,$=\$35{,}100$).
The primary error is a \$36{,}300 overstatement of Cash:

\begin{small}
\begin{verbatim}
Ground truth:            Model output:
"Cash": 76300            "Cash": 112600   [+36300]
"Prepaid Insurance":1100 "Prepaid Insurance":1100
"Equipment": 56500       "Equipment": 56400 [-100]
"Inventory": 6600        "Inventory": 6600
"Furniture": 14200       "Furniture": 14200
"Vehicles": 46100        "Vehicles": 46100
  Total assets: 200800     Total assets: 237000
\end{verbatim}
\end{small}

\noindent
Assets are overstated by \$36{,}200 while liabilities and equity are
correct in structure but the balance equation
$A = L + E$ is violated by \$35{,}100.
This pattern---structurally complete but numerically
incoherent---typifies the ``arithmetic collapse'' failure mode, where
the model recovers the right account names but loses track of running
balances across many transactions.

\paragraph{Retained Earnings omission (closing-entry blindness).}
DeepSeek-v3.2 under \zs{} on problem FB\_2\_00851 (Level~2)
omits Retained Earnings entirely (\cecr{}\,$=0$):

\begin{small}
\begin{verbatim}
Ground truth equity:     Model output equity:
"Owner's Equity": 56700  "Owner's Equity": 56800
"Retained Earnings":1700  (missing)
\end{verbatim}
\end{small}

\noindent
The model recognizes the correct number of asset and liability
accounts (\acr{}\,$=0.86$) but never closes revenue and expense
activity into Retained Earnings.  Instead, the net income is
partially absorbed into a slightly inflated Owner's Equity, destroying
both account-level accuracy and the balance equation.  This is the
single most frequent failure in the benchmark, with Retained Earnings
showing a 70.4\% omission rate across all models.

\paragraph{Commission error (unclosed income-statement accounts).}
DeepSeek-v3.2 under \zs{} on problem FB\_3\_01353 (Level~3) places
five income-statement accounts directly into the equity section instead
of closing them into Retained Earnings:

\begin{small}
\begin{verbatim}
Ground truth equity:     Model output equity:
"Owner's Equity": 38900  "Owner's Equity": 38900
"Retained Earnings":35100 "Revenue": 48900
                          "Operating Expenses":-2500
                          "Cost of Goods Sold":-10400
                          "Bad Debt Expense": -700
                          "Interest Expense": -400
\end{verbatim}
\end{small}

\noindent
This is a commission error: the five income-statement line items
(Revenue, Operating Expenses, COGS, Bad Debt Expense, Interest
Expense) should not appear on the balance sheet at all.  Their net
effect should be a single Retained Earnings entry of \$35{,}100.
The model has skipped the closing-entry step entirely, exposing
temporary accounts as if they were permanent equity items.

\paragraph{Formatting failure (non-numeric expression).}
Llama~3.3~70B under \zs{} on problem FB\_1\_00480 (Level~1) emits an
arithmetic expression instead of a computed value:

\begin{small}
\begin{verbatim}
"equity": {
  "Owner's Equity": 29000+3200-2200-9400-5400-6700
}
\end{verbatim}
\end{small}

\noindent
While the model identifies the right account and even enumerates
the correct adjustments, the result is not valid JSON because the
value is an expression rather than a number.  The harness marks this
as a parse failure and retries with a \zs{} fallback prompt.
Such formatting errors account for \(17\%\) of gpt-oss-120b \zs{}
outputs and a smaller fraction of Llama runs.
