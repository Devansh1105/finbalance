\section{Appendix}

\subsection{Worked Pipeline Example}
\label{app:worked-example}

This appendix gives a concrete end-to-end example of the benchmark
pipeline, from prompted input to ledger processing to the expected
JSON answer.

\paragraph{Prompted input.}
\begin{verbatim}
Opening balances:
Cash 49,400; Owner's Equity 49,400

Transactions:
1. Dr Equipment 17,700 / Cr Cash 17,700
2. Dr Operating Expenses 200 / Cr Cash 200
3. Dr Prepaid Rent 5,900 / Cr Cash 5,900
4. Dr Prepaid Rent 3,400 / Cr Cash 3,400
5. Dr Cash 20,600 /
   Cr Owner's Equity 20,600
\end{verbatim}

\paragraph{Ledger processing.}
\begin{center}
\small
\begin{tabularx}{\columnwidth}{@{}l>{\raggedright\arraybackslash}X@{}}
\toprule
Step & Running balances and effect \\
\midrule
Opening & Cash \(=49{,}400\); Owner's Equity \(=49{,}400\). \\
Tx 1 & Purchase equipment for cash. Cash decreases to \(31{,}700\); Equipment increases to \(17{,}700\). \\
Tx 2 & Record operating expense. Cash decreases to \(31{,}500\); Operating Expenses \(=200\). \\
Tx 3 & Record prepaid rent. Cash decreases to \(25{,}600\); Prepaid Rent \(=5{,}900\). \\
Tx 4 & Record additional prepaid rent. Cash decreases to \(22{,}200\); Prepaid Rent increases to \(9{,}300\). \\
Tx 5 & Owner contributes additional cash. Cash increases to \(42{,}800\); Owner's Equity increases to \(70{,}000\). \\
Closing & Expense accounts close into equity. Operating Expenses \(=200\) therefore become Retained Earnings \(=-200\). \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabularx}
\end{center}

\paragraph{Expected output.}
\begin{verbatim}
{
  "assets": {
    "Cash": 42800,
    "Equipment": 17700,
    "Prepaid Rent": 9300
  },
  "liabilities": {},
  "equity": {
    "Owner's Equity": 70000,
    "Retained Earnings": -200
  }
}
\end{verbatim}

The final statement balances because total assets equal
\(42{,}800 + 17{,}700 + 9{,}300 = 69{,}800\), while total equity
equals \(70{,}000 - 200 = 69{,}800\) and liabilities are zero.
This example makes explicit the accounting behaviors the benchmark
tests: transaction accumulation, section placement, and closing of
income-statement activity into Retained Earnings.

\subsection{Evaluation Protocol Details}

The benchmark uses a deterministic synthetic generator, a stratified held-out split, and structured JSON scoring.
All reported runs ask the model to return three top-level sections: \texttt{assets}, \texttt{liabilities}, and \texttt{equity}.
If a response contains reasoning or markdown wrappers, the parser extracts the final parseable JSON object before scoring.

\subsection{Prompted Output Format}

All reported runs ask the model to emit a JSON object with three top-level keys:
\texttt{assets}, \texttt{liabilities}, and \texttt{equity}.
\CoT{} prompts may include intermediate reasoning, but the final score is based only on the terminal structured answer.
