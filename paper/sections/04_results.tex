Among completed Large-500 runs, DeepSeek-v3.2 \CoT{} is the strongest
system with \fbs{} \(=92.25\) and \ba{} \(=99.4\%\).
Its lead over the second-ranked Grok~4.1 Fast \CoT{} is \(+3.26\) \fbs{}
points (\(p<0.001\)), and its lead over Gemini~3 Flash \CoT{} is
\(+3.42\) points (\(p<0.001\)).
Grok~4.1 Fast \CoT{} and Gemini~3 Flash \CoT{} are statistically
indistinguishable (\(\Delta=0.15\), \(p=0.71\)), despite Grok achieving
higher balance accuracy (\(99.2\%\) vs.\ \(96.2\%\)) while Gemini
achieves higher account-level accuracy (\(78.8\%\) vs.\ \(76.4\%\)).
GPT-5.4 Nano \CoT{} and Qwen~3.6 Plus \CoT{} also form a
statistically indistinguishable pair (\(\Delta=0.16\), \(p=0.81\)),
both ranking just below the Grok/Gemini tier.
On the separate Controlled-100 compatibility pool, GPT-5.2 remains very
strong (\(90.85\) under \CoT{}, \(90.20\) under \zs{}), extending the
coverage summary to an additional frontier model family evaluated on the
smaller matched pool.

Figure~\ref{fig:capability-curve} clarifies the Large-500 hierarchy.
DeepSeek-v3.2 \CoT{} is the only run with a reverse difficulty curve:
its mean \fbs{} rises from \(87.74\) at Level~1 to \(93.30\) at
Level~5.
Grok~4.1 Fast \CoT{} has the lowest difficulty-curve coefficient of
variation (CV\(=2.0\%\)), while GPT-5.4 Nano \CoT{} and Gemini~3 Flash
\CoT{} also remain relatively stable.
The zero-shot runs of all models except Gemini~3 Flash show cliff-like
degradation after Level~1, with GPT-5.4 Nano \zs{} the lowest-scoring
zero-shot performer (\fbs{} \(=31.57\)).

\paragraph{Structural recall is easier than numerical consistency.}
Several models achieve high account coverage rates under zero-shot
prompting while still recording low balance accuracy.
DeepSeek-v3.2 \zs{} reaches \acr{} \(=96.6\%\) but \ba{} \(=11.6\%\);
Nemotron~3 Super \zs{} shows \acr{} \(=89.3\%\) with \ba{} \(=25.2\%\);
and Grok~4.1 Fast \zs{} reaches \acr{} \(=88.1\%\) with \ba{}
\(=6.2\%\).
This pattern reflects a distinct failure mode: high structural fidelity
combined with arithmetic collapse.

\paragraph{Prompt sensitivity is highly model-dependent.}
The Large-500 paired tests reveal a wide range of \CoT{} benefit sizes.
GPT-5.4 Nano gains \(+55.52\) \fbs{} points from \zs{} to \CoT{},
Grok~4.1 Fast gains \(+45.33\), DeepSeek-v3.2 gains \(+41.43\),
Qwen~3.5 Flash gains \(+36.40\), and Gemini~3 Flash gains a more
modest \(+8.63\).
Gemini's gain is also difficulty-conditional rather than uniform:
\CoT{} is slightly worse at Level~1 (\(-0.97\) \fbs{}), but strongly
better at Level~4 (\(+14.35\)) and Level~5 (\(+18.91\)).
Prompt sensitivity is therefore not a single scalar property of a model
family; it depends on which accounting mechanisms become active as
difficulty rises.
