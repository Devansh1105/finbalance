\section{Main Results}

\input{tables/main_results}

Table~\ref{tab:main-results} reports the paired cross-model comparison
on the shared 100-problem split (\(n=100\)).
Even in that matched setting, the performance spread is wide.
GPT-5.2 is clearly strongest and is the only family that remains near
the oracle ceiling in both prompt settings.
Its best run, GPT-5.2 \CoT{}, reaches perfect balance accuracy and a
FinBalance Score of \(90.85\).
The corresponding \zs{} run is effectively tied: the paired
permutation test reports a mean score difference of only \(+0.65\)
points with two-sided \(p=0.533\).

Figure~\ref{fig:capability-curve} makes the performance hierarchy more
interpretable.
Panel~(A) shows that GPT-5.2 is nearly flat across difficulty, whereas
all other models degrade visibly at Levels~4 and~5.
Panel~(B) shows that this degradation is not merely a mild score drop:
for several models it corresponds to a sharp collapse in equation
satisfaction.
Among the open-weight systems, the strongest runs are
Qwen~3.5 Flash \CoT{} (score \(=86.72\)),
gpt-oss-120b \CoT{} (score \(=86.35\)), and
DeepSeek-v3.2 \CoT{} (score \(=85.56\)).
Gemini 3 Flash forms the strongest non-GPT closed-source baseline in
the controlled study, while Llama~3.3~70B remains weak even with
\CoT{}.

\paragraph{Structural recall is easier than numerical consistency.}
The most diagnostic row in Table~\ref{tab:main-results} is
DeepSeek-v3.2 \zs{}.
Its account coverage rate reaches \(96.8\%\), which means it usually
predicts the expected account set, yet its balance accuracy is only
\(15\%\) and its FinBalance Score falls to \(52.96\).
Instead, the model displays a qualitatively distinct failure mode:
high structural fidelity combined with arithmetic collapse.

\paragraph{Prompt sensitivity is highly model-dependent.}
Gemini 3 Flash improves modestly under \CoT{} (\(+4.43\) score
points), and the paired permutation test labels that gain marginal
under a two-sided test (\(p=0.062\)).
DeepSeek-v3.2, by contrast, improves by \(+32.6\) score points and
\(+74\) balance-accuracy points.
This is the largest prompt-induced gain in the benchmark and indicates
that the model's failure under \zs{} is not lack of financial
vocabulary but failure to activate a stable accounting procedure.
