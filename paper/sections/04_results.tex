Table~\ref{tab:main-results} shows a broad spread in performance even though every system receives the same structured task.
GPT-5.2 is clearly strongest and is the only family that remains near the oracle ceiling in both prompt settings.
Its best run, GPT-5.2 \CoT{}, reaches perfect balance accuracy and \fbs{} $=90.85$.
The corresponding \zs{} run is effectively tied: the retained paired permutation test reports a mean \fbs{} difference of only \(+0.65\) points with two-sided \(p=0.533\).

Figure~\ref{fig:capability-curve} makes the performance hierarchy more interpretable.
GPT-5.2 is nearly flat across difficulty, while all other models show increasingly visible degradation at Levels~4 and~5.
Among non-frontier systems, the strongest runs are Qwen~3.5 Flash \CoT{} (\fbs{} $=86.72$), gpt-oss-120b \CoT{} (\fbs{} $=86.35$), and DeepSeek-v3.2 \CoT{} (\fbs{} $=85.56$).
Gemini 3 Flash occupies a middle band, and Llama~3.3~70B remains weak even with \CoT{}.

\paragraph{Structural recall is easier than numerical consistency.}
The most diagnostic row in Table~\ref{tab:main-results} is DeepSeek-v3.2 \zs{}.
Its \acr{} reaches \(96.8\%\), which means it usually predicts the expected account set, yet its \ba{} is only \(15\%\) and its \fbs{} falls to \(52.96\).
This is not a parsing problem, since its parse-failure rate is \(0\%\).
Instead, the model displays a qualitatively distinct failure mode: high structural fidelity combined with arithmetic collapse.

\paragraph{Prompt sensitivity is highly model-dependent.}
Gemini 3 Flash improves modestly under \CoT{} (\(+4.43\) \fbs{} points), and the retained permutation test labels that gain marginal under a two-sided test (\(p=0.062\)).
DeepSeek-v3.2, by contrast, improves by \(+32.6\) \fbs{} points and \(+74\) \ba{} points.
This is the largest prompt-induced gain in the benchmark and indicates that the model's failure under \zs{} is not lack of financial vocabulary but failure to activate a stable accounting procedure.
