\section{Main Results}

Table~\ref{tab:main-results} reports the two evaluation pools used in
the current paper.
Panel~A is the completed Large-500 study and supports the main
comparative claims.
Panel~B reports Controlled-100 compatibility runs for frontier systems
that were not rerun to completion on the larger subset.
Because the pools differ in both coverage and sample size, we do not use
Table~\ref{tab:main-results} to rank models across panels.

Among completed Large-500 runs, DeepSeek-v3.2 \CoT{} is the strongest
system with \fbs{} \(=92.25\) and \ba{} \(=99.4\%\).
Its lead over Gemini~3 Flash \CoT{} is \(+3.42\) \fbs{} points under a
paired permutation test with \(p<0.001\), and its lead over
Qwen~3.6 Plus \CoT{} is \(+5.33\) points with \(p<0.001\).
Qwen~3.6 Plus \CoT{} is the strongest non-DeepSeek open model on the
Large-500 subset, outperforming Qwen~3.5 Flash \CoT{} by \(+4.01\)
\fbs{} points (\(p<0.001\)).
On the separate Controlled-100 compatibility pool, GPT-5.2 remains very
strong (\(90.85\) under \CoT{}, \(90.20\) under \zs{}), but those
numbers are descriptive only relative to the Large-500 panel because
they were obtained on a different evaluation pool.

Figure~\ref{fig:capability-curve} makes the Large-500 hierarchy more
interpretable.
DeepSeek-v3.2 \CoT{} is the only run with a reverse difficulty curve:
its mean \fbs{} rises from \(87.74\) at Level~1 to \(93.30\) at
Level~5.
Gemini~3 Flash \CoT{} is also robust, but it falls back at Level~5,
while Qwen~3.6 Plus \CoT{} separates itself from Qwen~3.5 Flash mainly
on the hardest problems.
The zero-shot runs of DeepSeek-v3.2 and Qwen~3.5 Flash show the opposite
pattern: after Level~1, balance accuracy largely collapses.

\paragraph{Structural recall is easier than numerical consistency.}
The most diagnostic row in Table~\ref{tab:main-results} is
DeepSeek-v3.2 \zs{}.
On the Large-500 subset its account coverage rate reaches \(96.6\%\),
which means it usually predicts the expected account set, yet its
balance accuracy is only \(11.6\%\) and its FinBalance Score falls to
\(50.82\).
Its mean balance-error magnitude is also enormous
(\(\textsc{BEM}=\$61{,}339.2\)).
Instead, the model displays a qualitatively distinct failure mode:
high structural fidelity combined with arithmetic collapse.

\paragraph{Prompt sensitivity is highly model-dependent.}
The Large-500 paired tests show three very different \CoT{} regimes.
DeepSeek-v3.2 gains \(+41.43\) \fbs{} points from \zs{} to \CoT{},
Qwen~3.5 Flash gains \(+36.40\), and Gemini~3 Flash gains a much
smaller \(+8.63\).
Gemini's gain is also difficulty-conditional rather than uniform:
\CoT{} is slightly worse at Level~1 (\(-0.97\) \fbs{}), but strongly
better at Level~4 (\(+14.35\)) and Level~5 (\(+18.91\)).
This contrast suggests that prompt sensitivity is not a single scalar
property of a model family; it depends on which accounting mechanisms
become active as difficulty rises.
