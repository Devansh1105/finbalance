\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related}

\paragraph{Financial-NLP question answering.}
FinQA \citep{chen-etal-2021-finqa} and ConvFinQA \citep{chen-etal-2022-convfinqa} pose numerical questions over single financial reports and their tabular content, with the latter requiring conversational multi-turn reasoning. TAT-QA \citep{zhu-etal-2021-tatqa} similarly evaluates models on hybrid tabular-and-text questions over filings. DocFinQA \citep{reddy-etal-2024-docfinqa} extends this paradigm to longer financial documents, and MultiHiertt \citep{zhao-etal-2022-multihiertt} targets reasoning over multiple hierarchical tables and accompanying text. These benchmarks share the assumption that a structured artifact---a table extracted from a filing, a single report---already exists, and the model's task is to answer questions \emph{over} it. \benchmark{} differs structurally: there is no prepared statement, only a bundle of raw source documents with OCR text from which the statement must be constructed.

\paragraph{Financial benchmark suites.}
FinanceBench \citep{islam2023financebench}, FinanceMATH \citep{zhao-etal-2024-financemath}, FinBen \citep{xie2024finben}, FinDABench \citep{liu-etal-2025-findabench}, FinTagging \citep{wang2025fintagging}, FinRule-Bench \citep{malarkkan2026finrulebench}, FinMaster \citep{jiang2025finmaster}, and FinAuditing \citep{wang2026finauditing} broaden financial evaluation to filings, math, tagging, taxonomy-structured auditing, accounting principles, and workflow-style tasks (Table~\ref{tab:prior-work-comparison}). They still evaluate answers, tags, judgments, or prepared-statement reasoning rather than requiring models to construct a cited double-entry ledger and balance sheet from source-document bundles. \benchmark{} fills this gap: its ground truth is a computed ledger, and the consistency between journal entries and their aggregated balance sheet is directly measurable.

\paragraph{Self-consistency, refinement, and verifiers.}
Self-consistency \citep{wang2023selfconsistency} samples multiple chains; Self-Refine \citep{madaan2023selfrefine} and Reflexion \citep{shinn2023reflexion} use language-model feedback; trained verifiers for math \citep{cobbe2021gsm8k,lightman2024verify} require labeled supervision. We do not propose a new general verifier method. Instead, we use the benchmark's deterministic ledger in a diagnostic ablation: compare a model's reported balance sheet to the one implied by its own entries, then test whether one feedback turn changes the result.

\paragraph{Long-context, document evidence, and tool use.}
\citet{liu2024lostmiddle} document a U-shaped accuracy curve as the position of relevant evidence varies within long contexts---directly relevant to our context-stress findings (\S\ref{sec:results}), where placing supporting documents at the end of the document bundle differentially affects inconsistency detection. Retrieval-augmented approaches \citep{lewis2020rag} address related document-grounding challenges; our \emph{evidence-only} ablation reports an oracle-retrieval upper bound. Finally, ReAct \citep{yao2023react} demonstrated the value of interleaving reasoning with tool calls; we observe in our setting that models bypass offered tools and emit final answers directly, so forced feedback is included only as a diagnostic ablation.
