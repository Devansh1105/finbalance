\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

LLMs are increasingly applied to financial tasks, but the dominant evaluation paradigm targets \emph{comprehension} of already-prepared artifacts. FinQA \citep{chen-etal-2021-finqa}, ConvFinQA \citep{chen-etal-2022-convfinqa}, TAT-QA \citep{zhu-etal-2021-tatqa}, and DocFinQA \citep{reddy-etal-2024-docfinqa} pose numerical questions over excerpted tables and text. FinanceBench \citep{islam2023financebench}, FinanceMATH \citep{zhao-etal-2024-financemath}, FinBen \citep{xie2024finben}, and FinAuditing \citep{wang2026finauditing} test analysis or auditing over prepared statements. In each case the financial statement already exists; the model reasons \emph{over} it.

Real accounting workflows begin one step earlier. A packet of source documents---invoices, bank statements, payment notices, contracts, schedules, exemption certificates---must be reconciled: each transaction extracted as a balanced journal entry citing its supporting documents, the entries aggregated into a balance sheet that respects the accounting equation, and contradictions among documents detected and classified rather than silently resolved. We introduce \benchmark{}, a multi-document accounting reconciliation benchmark of OCR-style packets across eight industries, three period types, and five difficulty levels, with concept-coverage flags for revenue recognition under ASC~606, leases, deferred tax, asset disposals, multi-jurisdictional sales tax, foreign-currency remeasurement, and tax exemptions.

\benchmark{} is synthetic, but the synthetic step is the final composition step rather than the source of the accounting knowledge. We first author the business scenarios, document schemas, chart of accounts, accounting policies, tax and FX treatments, support-document roles, distractor templates, and contradiction templates. The generator then mixes these human-defined components, samples dates and values, renders OCR-style documents, and replays the fixed double-entry ledger to compute labels. This is close to how a human would construct analogous benchmark packets by hand; the code removes the repetitive sampling and arithmetic while keeping the business logic explicit and inspectable. Given that fixed specification, every ground-truth label---entries, balance sheet, opening trial balance, and 23 inconsistency codes---is computable rather than hand-annotated. The same generator can sample additional records along any stratification axis, providing fine-tuning corpora, targeted concept probes, and held-out evaluation sets at scales annotation cannot reach.

We report two complementary metrics. \bsexact{} measures whether the model's own self-reported balance sheet matches ground truth exactly; \bsrecon{} measures whether the balance sheet obtained by deterministically replaying the model's own journal entries matches. The two diverge when intermediate entries are reconcilable but the self-aggregated balance sheet is not. Evaluating six frontier LLMs (Gemini~3 Flash, GPT-5-low, Claude Haiku~4.5, Grok-4.3, DeepSeek V3.2, Qwen~3 235B) yields three findings. \textbf{(i)}~An \aggGap{}: four of six show $25$--$38$\,pp between \bsrecon{} and \bsexact{}. The remaining two lack the gap for qualitatively different reasons---GPT-5-low is genuinely self-consistent and reaches the highest \bsexact{} (51\%), while Grok-4.3 attains apparent consistency by under-answering (submitting only 40\% of expected entries, \S\ref{sec:analysis}). \textbf{(ii)}~Document-linking is the dominant entry-level failure: 52\% of journal entries with correct account and amount cite the wrong documents, a rate that barely moves under explicit citation-pressure prompting ($-1.4$\,pp), implicating retrieval-disambiguation rather than prompt compliance. \textbf{(iii)}~Given the fixed benchmark specification, a rule-based \verifier{} comparing the model's reported balance sheet against the one its own entries imply, returning ledger deltas as one-shot feedback, closes the aggregation gap by $+25$ to $+37$\,pp \bsexact{} across four model families with no fine-tuning---demonstrating the failure is process-level, not parametric \citep{wang2023selfconsistency,madaan2023selfrefine}. The same verifier degrades inconsistency-code detection by $-13$ to $-61$\,pp on smaller models; we report this trade-off rather than suppress it.

\textbf{Contributions.}
\textbf{(1)}~A multi-document accounting reconciliation benchmark with deterministic, fully computable ground truth and 23 inconsistency codes, released with the generator so additional records can be sampled at will. \textbf{(2)}~A multi-model evaluation revealing a self-consistency \aggGap{}, with the strongest model reaching only 51\% \bsexact{}. \textbf{(3)}~A rule-based, training-free \verifier{} that closes the gap across four model families, with honest disclosure of an inconsistency-detection trade-off on smaller models. \textbf{(4)}~Expert validation by practising finance professionals, including an independently double-reviewed agreement subset.
