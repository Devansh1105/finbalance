\section{Conclusion}

\benchmark{} turns balance-sheet construction into a controlled
structured-generation benchmark with exact ground truth.
Modern language models differ not only in final score but in failure
type: some miss accounts, some preserve structure while breaking the
accounting equation, and even strong systems remain challenged on
retained-earnings closure.
Chain-of-thought prompting is strongly model-dependent, with large gains for GPT-5.4 Nano,
Grok~4.1 Fast, and DeepSeek-v3.2 and smaller gains for
Gemini~3 Flash.
Account-level and factor-level analysis are therefore necessary to
interpret leaderboard scores, and separating Large-500 from
Controlled-100 avoids misleading cross-pool rankings.
\benchmark{} is most useful as an executable testbed for stateful,
constraint-governed generation in a high-stakes domain.
