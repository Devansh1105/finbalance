\section{Conclusion}

\benchmark{} turns balance-sheet preparation and verification into a controlled structured-generation benchmark with exact ground truth, reusable analysis tooling, and paper-ready artifacts.
The experiments show that modern LLMs differ sharply not only in final score, but in \emph{how} they fail.
Some models miss the right accounts, some preserve structure but break the balance equation, and some nearly solve the task while remaining blind to Retained Earnings.

Two conclusions stand out.
First, prompt sensitivity is model-specific: \CoT{} is transformative for DeepSeek-v3.2, modest for Gemini and Qwen, and nearly irrelevant for GPT-5.2.
Second, account-level and factor-level analysis is necessary to interpret leaderboard scores.
Without it, one would miss both the structural-versus-numerical split seen in DeepSeek-v3.2 \zs{} and the central role of mixed-funding transactions and closing-entry behavior in separating robust systems from brittle ones.
