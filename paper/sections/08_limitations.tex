\section*{Limitations}

\benchmark{} is synthetic: every document is rendered from a generator. The important qualification is that the generator composes human-authored business scenarios, document schemas, accounting policies, tax and FX assumptions, support-document dependencies, distractors, and contradiction templates; it does not discover accounting logic automatically. This is the deliberate trade for computable ground truth at scale. The cost is that the benchmark cannot evaluate OCR robustness or real-document idiosyncrasies. Our human validation (\S\ref{sec:human-validation}) addresses analogy and plausibility, not real-document fidelity. Headline numbers use a single decoding seed at temperature $0$; differences smaller than $\sim$3\,pp should be treated as not robust. Six models are evaluated at full $n{=}143$ on baseline; verifier replication is full on three and partial ($n{=}85$) on Qwen. The aggregation gap is established in four of six frontier models, not asserted universally. The \verifier{} degrades inconsistency-code detection on smaller models, as reported. Human validation covers a 75-record expert-reviewed release subset with a 25-record independent overlap for agreement; the overlap has skewed positive marginals, so raw agreement is more interpretable than Cohen's $\kappa$.

\paragraph{Ethics.} The benchmark uses synthetic data; no real records, PII, or production accounting data are used. Reviewers participated on a volunteer basis. We follow the ACL Responsible NLP Research checklist (Appendix~\ref{appx:reproducibility}).
