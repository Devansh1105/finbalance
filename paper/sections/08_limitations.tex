\section{Limitations}
\label{sec:limitations}

\benchmark{} is synthetic: every document is rendered from a deterministic generator. This is the deliberate trade for computable ground truth at scale; the cost is that the benchmark cannot evaluate OCR robustness or real-document idiosyncrasies. Our human validation (\S\ref{sec:human-validation}) addresses analogy and plausibility, not real-document fidelity. Headline numbers use a single decoding seed at temperature $0$; differences smaller than $\sim$3\,pp should be treated as not robust. Six models are evaluated at full $n{=}143$ on baseline; verifier replication is full on three and partial ($n{=}85$) on Qwen. The aggregation gap is established in four of six frontier models, not asserted universally. The \verifier{} degrades inconsistency-code detection on smaller models, as reported. Of the 75 records in the validation packet, 51 received double-reviewed sign-off; the remaining 24 are reserved for an extended camera-ready pass.

\paragraph{Ethics.} The benchmark uses synthetic data; no real records, PII, or production accounting data are used. Reviewers participated on a volunteer basis. We follow the ACL Responsible NLP Research checklist (Appendix~\ref{appx:reproducibility}).
